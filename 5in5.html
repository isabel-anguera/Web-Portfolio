Creating 5 HTML sketches in five days following two preset constraints.

The Constraints

1. In your sketch, only use colors (and their hues) that can be found on the first person (who you know) you see each day’s outfit.


2. Make the function of your sketch something that would satisfy however Google says you are feeling.

Day 1

Outfit of the Day: Blue dress + white tennis shoes

Feeling of the Day: Adventurous

What It Is: This is a simple sketch in which you can click the 'Give Me an Idea' button if you're feeling adventurous but don't know what to do. There are about 20 suggestions that will be picked randomly.

Visit Site

Day 2

Outfit of the Day: Maroon polo + khacki shorts + black shoes

Feeling of the Day: Artistic

What It Is: This sketch makes the mouse a sort of paint brush which you can use to make any sort of line drawing on the page.

Visit Site

Day 3

Outfit of the Day: Violet polo + green and brown shorts + brown shoes

Feeling of the Day: Trendy

What It Is: The colors of the outfit of the day were tougher to work with--in the sketch that came out of it I created big buttons with titles of things that could be considered trendy, and linked them to a related pinterest page.

Visit Site

Day 4

Outfit of the Day: Grey shirt + black sweater + blue jeans + beige shoes + gold jewelry

Feeling of the Day: Curious

What It Is: This is a grid of fun fact questions that you can find the answer to by rolling over them with your mouse.

Visit Site

Day 5

Outfit of the Day: Steelblue pants + white shirt + black sweater + white shoes + gold jewelry

Feeling of the Day: Generous

What It Is: This sketch is a portal to some of the major Democratic primary candidate's donation pages. I image-traced the candidates as the buttons and placed them in a grid formation by alphabetical last name.

Visit Site

Takeaways

Picking the right constraints to work by, or finding the right way to work within specifically tough constraints is an important skill to have because they can guide the way that your work flows.
